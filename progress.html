Progress

Abstract

  • This portfolio website contains five bootstrap responsive components including the carousel, collapse, card, navbar, and form text.
  • The website includes a home page and four inner pages “About Me”, “Projects”, “Progress” and “Contact Me”.
  • Four typologies including home/landing page typology, content-heavy page typology, photo-rich page typology, and form page typology are introduced on this website.

Detailed Progress

The goal of creating this website is to show my professionality in UX/UI design, graphic design and front-end development. The primary target audience for this portfolio website is those who are potentially hiring me to be their employee or someone who is looking forward to cooperating with me. I’ve done a lot of research before creating my portfolio website. At the beginning of the project, I started with building an information architecture and wireframe for all the pages.

The Home page used a Home/Landing page typology, characterized by the introduction of the website as a whole while guiding the user through to the corresponsive inner pages when they are interested in a particular section. Users can preview my basic biography information, professional skills, tech stacks, and projects and contact me on the homepage. Instead of going back to the navigation bar, users have the accessibility to the inner pages by clicking the button just below the preview information.


“About Me” page uses content page typography, characterized by a balanced mix of text and images, introducing my biography, and relative information about me like curriculum and hobby. The overall page helps the user to start getting to know me.


The “Projects” page is a photo-rich typology with minimum text because this page is a gallery to show my previously designed work.


The progress page is content-heavy typology because it is ideally used to explain the rationale for design selections. I also placed wireframe graphs as an illustration.


The “Contact Me” page is a form page typology featured with contacting information, I used the “form text” coding framework from Bootstrap, with a needs-validation feature. In addition, embedded a form-check box to make the page more interactive.


To make the website a unique and fashionable design, I’ve put effort into the colour chosen, element selection and combination of layouts. I’ve seen most of the designer portfolio website is backgrounded in white or black colour. To make my portfolio an exception, I used a combination of dark blue and grey-blue as the portfolio colour theme. According to my research, blue is a colour that refers to technology and intelligence. Many technology companies also used blue as their logo colour and website page colour.

After I evaluating my portfolio, the three primary skills are all about design, including graphic design, UX/UI design and front-end website design. Therefore, the shortcut icon I designed for my portfolio combines the first two letters in my name and the letter D, referring to “design.” The colour of the icon uses dark blue and light blue.
